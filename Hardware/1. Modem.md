# Motorola MB7621

The Motorola MB7621 is the entry point of my home network. As a DOCSIS 3.0 cable modem, it converts the incoming coaxial cable connection from my ISP into an Ethernet connection for my firewall.

Although my current internet plan is 1 Gbps, the MB7621 is capable of real-world speeds of around 900 Mbps, making it the primary bottleneck in the network. Despite this limitation, it has been a reliable modem and was an excellent value for the price. Replacing it with a DOCSIS 3.1 modem is one of the next planned upgrades for this homelab.

## Pros

- Affordable compared to many DOCSIS 3.1 modems
- Reliable Broadcom chipset
- Stable performance with minimal maintenance
- Compatible with many major cable ISPs
- Eliminates monthly modem rental fees
- Works well for internet plans up to roughly 900 Mbps

## Cons

- Bottlenecks my current 1 Gbps internet plan
- Limited to DOCSIS 3.0
- Will need to be replaced if I move to a fiber internet provider (fiber uses an Optical Network Terminal instead of a cable modem)
- No multi-gig Ethernet support
- Aging hardware with limited long-term future

## Why I Chose It

When I purchased the MB7621, it offered one of the best price-to-performance ratios for a DOCSIS 3.0 modem. While it no longer fully matches my internet plan, it has remained reliable and serves as a solid starting point for this homelab. As I continue upgrading the network, replacing it with a DOCSIS 3.1 modem will improve both throughput and future compatibility.
